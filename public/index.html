Hello! I'm Theresa W. Cheng, a PhD student at the University of Oregon

I’m a developmental cognitive neuroscientist studying stress, puberty, and social learning in adolescents.

I’m broadly interested in how adolescents learn from their social environments, how stress and puberty shape neurobiological systems supporting social learning and motivation, and the potential to leverage these understandings to support positive behavior change and academic success in vulnerable youth.

Currently, I’m an NIH/NCATS pre-doctoral fellow at the Oregon Clinical and Translational Research Institute and a Millennium Scholar with the Society for Research in Child Development. I also co-organize the Eugene, OR pod of 500 Women Scientists and represent my fellow graduate students in the Graduate Teaching Fellows Federation at the University of Oregon.
